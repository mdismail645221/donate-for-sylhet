Deterioration of flood situation in Sylhet, longing for relief

The overall flood situation in Sylhet has further deteriorated. The water of Surma and Kushiyara rivers has been flowing over the danger zone for eight days. Thousands of villages in 13 upazilas of the district, including Sylhet city, are still facing water shortage. As a result, many have become unemployed, on the other hand, the food stored in the house has run out. Many have also complained that flood-affected people are not getting adequate relief.

Due to heavy rains on Wednesday night, the water level in the rivers has increased a little and new areas have been flooded since Thursday morning. According to various sources including local administration and people's representatives, at least 10 to 12 lakh people are stranded in Sylhet. Banavasi people complain that there is still water in many houses. Many have left their homes and taken refuge safely. Some people are building a loft inside the house.

Another 1,000 metric tons of rice and Tk 3 crore will be allocated for the flood victims.

The government has allocated an additional 1,000 metric tonnes of rice, cash and Rs 3.20 crore through banks and 400 bundles of corrugated iron to be distributed as humanitarian aid to the people affected by various natural calamities including floods. The allocation was made by the Ministry of Disaster Management and Relief on Tuesday.

A total of 1,000 metric tons of rice has been allotted for the flood-hit Sylhet and Sunamganj districts at 500 rupees, 1 crore rupees at 2 crore rupees and a total of 400 bundles of corrugated iron at 200 bundles. Apart from this, a total of Tk 1 crore 20 lakh has been allocated for each bundle of corrugated iron by paying Tk 3,000 through account pay check.
